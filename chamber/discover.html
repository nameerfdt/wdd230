History

Folsom was incorporated in 1946. Folsom has connections to the Gold Rush, the Pony Express, the first railroad in the west and more. Enjoy a taste of histroy in Folsom's Historic District, where you'll find premier dining, shopping, and historic architecture.

Folson is famous for it's stunning bridges: Rainbow Bridge, Truss Bridge, Lake Natoma Crossing, Folsom Lake Crossing Bridge.

In 1895, the first long-distance transmission of electricity took place at the Folsom Powerhouse. The transmission occurred over 22 miles from Folsom to Sacramento.

Recreational Activities

Folsom is Distinctive by Nature. Enjoy more than 50 miles of paved recreational trails, Folsom Lake, Lake Natoma, and the serene American River. Mountatin biking, hiking, and paddleboarding are just a few of the popular activities of Folsom.

Arts and Culture

The Folson community participates in many arts and cultural programs and events. The Harris Center provides enrichment through a beautiful and state-of-the-art 80,000 square foot reginal arts center. Programs are avialable at the Folsom Art Center and The Gallery at 48 Natoma. You'll also notice public art all around the city.

Surrounding Attractions

Take advantage of Folsom's relatively close proximity to various California attractions for a quick day trip and weekend get away.

  • Lake Tahoe: 75 miles to enjoy hiking, camping, snow skiing and various other outdoor activities.
  • Yosemite National Park: 150 miles southeast featuring granite cliffs, waterfalls, giant sequoia trees
  • Lassen Volcanic National Park: 160 miles northeast featuring steam vents, mud pots, hot springs, and a unique volcanic landscape.
  • San Francisco: 2 to 2.5 hours to scenic views of the Pacific Ocean, Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Fisherman's Wharf, and numerous other points of interest.
